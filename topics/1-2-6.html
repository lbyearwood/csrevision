Paper 1

1.2.6 Hexadecimal number system

Answer each question, then reveal the hidden mark scheme underneath to check your response.

1) Convert the denary number 203 into hexadecimal. Show your working. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

Award up to 3 marks:

  • Correctly divides the number into 16s and units or converts through binary.
  • Identifies 203 as 12 remainder 11.
  • Correct answer: CB.
  • Accept valid working that reaches the correct answer.

2) Convert the hexadecimal number 7E into denary. Show your working. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

Award up to 3 marks:

  • Correctly identifies 7 as the sixteens value and E as 14.
  • Correct calculation: 7 x 16 + 14.
  • Correct answer: 126.
  • Accept valid working that reaches the correct answer.

3) Convert the binary number 10101100 into hexadecimal. Show your working. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

Award up to 3 marks:

  • Correctly splits the binary number into nibbles: 1010 and 1100.
  • Correctly converts 1010 to A and 1100 to C.
  • Correct answer: AC.
  • Accept valid working that reaches the correct answer.

4) Convert the hexadecimal number 3F into 8-bit binary. Show your working. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

Award up to 3 marks:

  • Correctly converts 3 into 0011.
  • Correctly converts F into 1111.
  • Correct answer: 00111111.
  • Accept valid working that reaches the correct answer.
