Answer each question, then reveal the hidden mark scheme underneath to check your response.

Mark scheme

Award up to 4 marks:

2) Add the two 8-bit binary numbers below and explain whether overflow has occurred. 11110000 + 00110000 (4 marks)

Mark scheme

Award up to 4 marks:

  • Correctly attempts binary addition.

3) Explain the meaning of the terms most significant bit and least significant bit in an 8-bit binary number. (4 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • The most significant bit is the leftmost bit in the binary number.
  • The most significant bit has the highest place value.
  • In an 8-bit positive binary number, the most significant bit represents 128.
  • The least significant bit is the rightmost bit in the binary number.
  • The least significant bit has the lowest place value.
  • In an 8-bit positive binary number, the least significant bit represents 1.
  • Accept any other valid point.

4) Explain the effect of shifting the binary number 00010110 two places to the left. (4 marks)

Mark scheme

Award up to 4 marks:

  • Correctly states that each left shift multiplies the number by 2.
  • Correctly states that shifting two places left multiplies the number by 4.
  • 00010110 is 22 and 01011000 is 88.
  • Accept any other valid point.
