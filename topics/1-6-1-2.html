Paper 1

1.6.1.2 Legislation

Answer each question, then reveal the hidden mark scheme underneath to check your response.

1) Explain why legislation is needed when using computer systems and digital data. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • Laws set rules for how technology and data can be used.
  • They help protect individuals, organisations and systems.
  • They can make misuse of computers or data illegal.
  • They give consequences for breaking rules, such as fines or prosecution.
  • Accept any other valid point.

2) Explain the purpose of data protection legislation. (4 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • Data protection legislation controls how personal data is collected, stored and used.
  • It helps protect people’s privacy.
  • Organisations must keep personal data secure and use it fairly/lawfully.
  • Individuals have rights over their personal data.
  • Organisations can face penalties if they mishandle personal data.
  • Accept any other valid point.

3) A school stores pupil names, addresses and medical information. Explain why data protection legislation is relevant. (4 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • The school is storing personal data about pupils.
  • Medical information is sensitive and must be protected carefully.
  • The school must keep the data secure and only use it for appropriate purposes.
  • The school must not share the data with unauthorised people.
  • Pupils/parents have rights over personal data.
  • Accept any other valid point.

4) Explain the purpose of computer misuse legislation. (4 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • Computer misuse legislation makes unauthorised access to computer systems illegal.
  • It covers hacking or trying to access systems without permission.
  • It can cover unauthorised modification of data or programs.
  • It can cover creating or spreading malware.
  • It helps protect computer systems from deliberate misuse.
  • Accept any other valid point.

5) A student guesses another user’s password and logs into their account without permission. Explain why this may be illegal. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • The student has accessed an account without permission.
  • Unauthorised access is covered by computer misuse legislation.
  • The student may be able to view or change data they are not allowed to access.
  • Guessing the password does not make the access authorised.
  • Accept any other valid point.

6) Explain the purpose of copyright legislation. (4 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • Copyright legislation protects original creative work.
  • It controls how work such as software, images, music, videos and text can be copied or shared.
  • It gives creators or rights holders control over the use of their work.
  • Using or distributing copyrighted work without permission may be illegal.
  • It helps creators receive credit or payment for their work.
  • Accept any other valid point.

7) A pupil downloads a paid software package from an unofficial website without paying. Explain which legislation may apply and why. (4 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • Copyright legislation may apply.
  • Software is protected by copyright.
  • Downloading or using paid software without permission/licence may be illegal.
  • The pupil may be using or copying the software without the rights holder’s permission.
  • Computer misuse or malware risks may also be relevant if the download is unsafe, if explained.
  • Accept any other valid point.

8) Compare data protection legislation and computer misuse legislation. (4 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • Data protection legislation focuses on how personal data is collected, stored and used.
  • Computer misuse legislation focuses on unauthorised access or damage to computer systems/data.
  • Data protection protects individuals’ personal information.
  • Computer misuse protects systems from actions such as hacking or malware.
  • Both help make the use of computer systems safer and more responsible.
  • Accept any other valid point.

9) Explain why organisations must consider copyright when using images from the Internet. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • Images may be protected by copyright.
  • The organisation may need permission or a suitable licence to use the image.
  • Using images without permission could break copyright law.
  • The organisation could face legal action, fines or reputational damage.
  • Accept any other valid point.

10) A business has a customer database. Explain three ways the business could follow data protection requirements. (6 marks)

Mark scheme

Use the banded mark scheme first, then the indicative content below to decide the final mark.

Mark range Quality of response Expected content
5-6 Clear, accurate and well-developed response. Applies the answer to the scenario or task and covers most of the relevant points from the indicative content.
3-4 Some accurate explanation with relevant points. Covers several relevant points, but the response may lack detail, balance or clear application.
1-2 Limited response with basic understanding. Includes one or two relevant points, but may be brief, generic or partly inaccurate.
0 No creditworthy response. No relevant answer, or the response is too unclear to award marks.

Indicative content:

  • Keep customer data secure using passwords, access levels or encryption.
  • Only collect data that is needed for a clear purpose.
  • Only use the data for the purpose customers were told about.
  • Keep the data accurate and up to date.
  • Delete data when it is no longer needed.
  • Do not share data with unauthorised people or organisations.
  • Allow customers to access or correct their personal data where appropriate.
  • Accept any other valid point.
