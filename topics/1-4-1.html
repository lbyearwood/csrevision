1.4.1 Threats to computer systems and networks questions

Answer each question, then reveal the hidden mark scheme underneath to check your response.

1) Define what is meant by malware. (2 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • Malware is malicious software.
  • It is designed to damage, disrupt, steal data from, or gain unauthorised access to a computer system.
  • Accept any other valid point.

2) Identify three types of malware. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct type:

  • Virus.
  • Worm.
  • Trojan.
  • Ransomware.
  • Spyware.
  • Adware.
  • Accept any other valid point.

3) A user opens an infected email attachment on a school laptop. Explain how a virus can affect the computer system. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • A virus attaches itself to files or programs.
  • It can spread when the infected file or program is run or shared.
  • It may delete, corrupt or modify data.
  • It may slow down the system or cause crashes.
  • Accept any other valid point.

4) Explain how a worm differs from a virus. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • A worm can spread across networks without needing to attach to a file.
  • A virus usually attaches to a file or program.
  • A worm can copy itself automatically.
  • Both are types of malware and can damage or disrupt systems.
  • Accept any other valid point.

5) A user receives an email that looks like it is from their bank and asks them to enter their password on a linked website. Explain what is meant by a phishing attack. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • Phishing is an attempt to trick a user into giving away sensitive information.
  • It often uses fake emails, messages or websites.
  • It may try to steal usernames, passwords or bank details.
  • The attacker may pretend to be a trusted organisation or person.
  • Accept any other valid point.

6) A user receives an email claiming their account will be closed unless they click a link and enter their password. Explain why this may be a phishing attack. (4 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • The email creates urgency or pressure.
  • It asks the user to click a link.
  • It asks for sensitive information such as a password.
  • The link may lead to a fake website.
  • The attacker may be pretending to be a trusted organisation.
  • Accept any other valid point.

7) Explain what is meant by social engineering. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • Social engineering manipulates people into giving access or information.
  • It targets human behaviour rather than only technical weaknesses.
  • Attackers may pretend to be trusted people or organisations.
  • It can be used to obtain passwords, personal information or access to systems.
  • Accept any other valid point.

8) Explain what is meant by a brute-force attack. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • A brute-force attack repeatedly tries different passwords or keys.
  • The attacker attempts many combinations until the correct one is found.
  • Weak or short passwords are more vulnerable to this attack.
  • It may be automated using software.
  • Accept any other valid point.

9) Explain what is meant by a denial of service attack. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • A denial of service attack tries to make a service unavailable to users.
  • It may flood a server or network with traffic or requests.
  • The system may slow down, crash or stop responding.
  • A distributed denial of service attack uses many devices to send traffic.
  • Accept any other valid point.

10) A customer enters payment details while using public Wi-Fi in a cafe. Explain how data interception can threaten the network communication. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • Data interception involves capturing data as it travels across a network.
  • Attackers may read sensitive information such as login details or personal data.
  • Unencrypted data is easier to understand if intercepted.
  • Interception can lead to identity theft, fraud or unauthorised access.
  • Accept any other valid point.

11) A business website becomes unavailable after receiving a very large number of fake requests. Identify the likely attack and explain your answer. (4 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • The likely attack is a denial of service or distributed denial of service attack.
  • The attacker sends a large number of requests or traffic.
  • The website/server becomes overloaded.
  • Legitimate users cannot access the service.
  • Accept any other valid point.

12) Explain how poor network security can affect an organisation. (6 marks)

Mark scheme

Use the banded mark scheme first, then the indicative content below to decide the final mark.

Mark range Quality of response Expected content
5-6 Clear, accurate and well-developed response. Applies the answer to the scenario or task and covers most of the relevant points from the indicative content.
3-4 Some accurate explanation with relevant points. Covers several relevant points, but the response may lack detail, balance or clear application.
1-2 Limited response with basic understanding. Includes one or two relevant points, but may be brief, generic or partly inaccurate.
0 No creditworthy response. No relevant answer, or the response is too unclear to award marks.

Indicative content:

  • Data may be stolen or changed.
  • Systems may be disrupted by malware or attacks.
  • The organisation may lose money due to downtime or recovery costs.

  • Staff may be unable to work if systems are unavailable.
  • Accept any other valid point.
