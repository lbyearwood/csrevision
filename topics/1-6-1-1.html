1.6.1.1 Ethical, legal, cultural, environmental and privacy issues questions

Answer each question, then reveal the hidden mark scheme underneath to check your response.

1) Define what is meant by an ethical issue in digital technology. (2 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • An ethical issue is about what is morally right or wrong.
  • It considers how technology affects people, society or behaviour.
  • Accept any other valid point.

2) Explain one privacy issue caused by digital technology. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • Personal data can be collected by websites, apps or organisations.
  • Users may not fully understand what data is collected or how it is used.
  • Data may be shared, sold, stolen or used without clear consent.
  • This can affect a person’s control over their personal information.
  • Accept any other valid point.

3) Explain one environmental issue caused by digital technology. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • Digital devices require energy to manufacture and use.
  • Data centres and networks use electricity and can increase carbon emissions.
  • Old devices can create e-waste if not recycled properly.
  • E-waste may contain harmful materials that damage the environment.
  • Accept any other valid point.

4) Explain one cultural issue caused by digital technology. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • Technology can change how people communicate, work or spend free time.
  • Online platforms can spread ideas, trends and content globally.
  • Some cultures or languages may become less visible online.
  • People may be influenced by content from other countries or communities.
  • Accept any other valid point.

5) Explain one legal issue linked to the use of digital technology. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • Organisations must follow laws about data protection, copyright or computer misuse.
  • Users may break the law if they access systems without permission.
  • Sharing copyrighted material without permission can be illegal.
  • Mishandling personal data can lead to legal penalties.
  • Accept any other valid point.

6) A company collects location data from users of its app. Explain two issues this could raise. (4 marks)

Mark scheme

Award up to 2 marks for each issue:

  • It raises a privacy issue because location data can reveal where users live, work or travel. [1] This may put users at risk if shared or stolen. [1]
  • It raises an ethical issue if users are not clearly told how the data will be used. [1] Users may not have given informed consent. [1]
  • It raises a legal issue if the company does not follow data protection laws. [1] The company may be fined or forced to change its practices. [1]
  • Accept any other valid point.

7) Explain how the digital divide can affect people. (4 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • The digital divide is the gap between people who have access to digital technology and those who do not.
  • People without access may struggle to complete schoolwork, apply for jobs or access services.
  • Lack of internet access or suitable devices can reduce opportunities.
  • It can increase inequality between individuals, groups or areas.
  • Accept any other valid point.

8) A school asks pupils to complete homework online. Explain why this could create a social or cultural issue. (4 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • Some pupils may not have reliable internet access at home.
  • Some pupils may not have a suitable device to use.
  • This could make it harder for them to complete homework compared with other pupils.
  • It could widen the digital divide or create unfairness.
  • The school may need to provide support or alternatives.
  • Accept any other valid point.

9) Explain one positive and one negative impact of digital technology on employment. (4 marks)

Mark scheme

Award up to 2 marks for each impact:

  • Digital technology can create new jobs. [1] For example, roles in cybersecurity, software development or data analysis. [1]
  • Digital technology can allow remote or flexible working. [1] This can improve access to employment for some people. [1]
  • Automation can replace some jobs. [1] Workers may need retraining or may lose employment. [1]
  • Digital monitoring can affect workers. [1] Staff may feel pressured or less trusted. [1]
  • Accept any other valid point.

10) Explain one issue caused by the collection and use of personal data. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • Personal data can be used to build profiles of users.
  • This may affect privacy or user autonomy.
  • Data could be used for targeted advertising or decision-making.
  • If the data is inaccurate or biased, users may be treated unfairly.
  • If the data is stolen, users may face fraud or identity theft.
  • Accept any other valid point.

11) Discuss the ethical, legal, cultural, environmental and privacy impacts of digital technology on society. (8 marks)

Mark scheme

Use the banded mark scheme first, then the indicative content below to decide the final mark.

Mark range Quality of response Expected content
7-8 Clear, accurate and well-developed response. Applies the answer to the scenario or task and covers most of the relevant points from the indicative content.
4-6 Some accurate explanation with relevant points. Covers several relevant points, but the response may lack detail, balance or clear application.
1-3 Limited response with basic understanding. Includes one or two relevant points, but may be brief, generic or partly inaccurate.
0 No creditworthy response. No relevant answer, or the response is too unclear to award marks.

Indicative content:

  • Level 3: The answer gives a developed discussion of several impacts of digital technology. It considers different categories such as ethical, legal, cultural, environmental and privacy issues. It makes clear points, includes examples and gives balanced consideration of benefits and drawbacks.
  • Level 2: The answer gives some relevant impacts of digital technology. It may cover more than one category but discussion is partly developed or examples are limited.
  • Level 1: The answer gives basic points about digital technology with little development. It may be mostly a list or focus on only one issue.
  • Ethical issues include fairness, consent, bias, surveillance and responsible use of technology.
  • Legal issues include data protection, copyright, computer misuse and responsibility for online actions.
  • Cultural issues include changes to communication, online behaviour, language, media and global influence.
  • Environmental issues include energy use, e-waste, resource use and carbon emissions from data centres.
  • Privacy issues include personal data collection, tracking, profiling, data sharing and breaches.
  • Positive impacts may include improved communication, access to information, flexible working and new jobs.
  • Negative impacts may include job loss, exclusion, cybercrime, misinformation or reduced privacy.
  • A developed answer links issues to specific examples and considers different stakeholders.
  • Accept any other valid point.
