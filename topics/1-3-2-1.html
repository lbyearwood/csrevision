1.3.2.1 Wired and wireless networks questions

Answer each question, then reveal the hidden mark scheme underneath to check your response.

1) Compare wired and wireless networks. (4 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • Wired networks use physical cables to transmit data.
  • Wireless networks transmit data using wireless signals/radio waves.
  • Wired networks are often faster or more stable.
  • Wireless networks allow devices to move around more easily.
  • Wireless networks can be affected by interference or range.
  • Wired networks may be less convenient because devices need a cable connection.
  • Accept any other valid point.

2) A school computer room uses desktop PCs for online assessments. Explain one advantage and one disadvantage of using a wired network in this room. (4 marks)

Mark scheme

Award up to 2 marks for each point:

  • Wired networks can be fast and reliable. [1] This is useful for transferring large files or stable connections. [1]
  • Wired networks can be more secure because physical access may be needed. [1] This can reduce the risk of unauthorised wireless access. [1]
  • Wired networks require cables. [1] This can make installation more expensive or less flexible. [1]
  • Users have less mobility. [1] Devices may need to stay near a network socket. [1]
  • Accept any other valid point.
