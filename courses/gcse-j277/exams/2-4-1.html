2.4.1 Logic gate diagrams questions

OCR-style practice on reading, drawing and correcting AND, OR and NOT logic gate diagrams.

1) Write the Boolean expression represented by Figure 1. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

  • Identifies A AND B.
  • Identifies that result is ORed with C.
  • Correct expression: Q = (A AND B) OR C.

2) Draw a logic gate diagram for Q = (A OR B) AND C. (4 marks)

Mark scheme

  • Inputs A and B are correctly labelled.
  • A and B enter an OR gate.
  • The OR output and C enter an AND gate.
  • The final output is labelled Q.

3) Draw a logic gate diagram for Q = (A AND NOT B) OR NOT C. (5 marks)

Mark scheme

  • Correctly labels inputs A, B and C.
  • Uses a NOT gate on B.
  • Combines A and NOT B using an AND gate.
  • Uses a NOT gate on C.
  • Combines the AND output and NOT C using an OR gate, with output Q.

4) A heater turns on only when the room is cold and the timer is active. Draw the logic diagram and name the gate used. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

  • Uses two inputs, cold and timer active.
  • Connects both inputs to an AND gate.
  • Labels the output as heater on, or equivalent.

5) Figure 2 shows Q = NOT((A AND B) OR C). Explain why the NOT gate is placed at the end of the diagram. (2 marks)

Mark scheme

  • The expression inside the brackets is processed first.
  • The final NOT gate inverts the whole result, not just one input.
