1.3.1 LAN vs WAN questions

Answer each question, then reveal the hidden mark scheme underneath to check your response.

1) Explain what is meant by a Local Area Network (LAN). (3 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • A LAN connects devices over a small geographical area.
  • It may cover a home, school or office.
  • The network hardware is usually owned or managed by the organisation or user.
  • Devices on a LAN can share resources such as files, printers or Internet access.
  • Accept any other valid point.

2) Explain what is meant by a Wide Area Network (WAN). (3 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • A WAN connects devices or LANs over a large geographical area.
  • A WAN can connect sites in different towns, countries or continents.
  • The Internet is an example of a WAN.
  • WANs often use infrastructure owned by telecommunications companies.
  • Accept any other valid point.

3) Compare a LAN and a WAN. (4 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • A LAN covers a small geographical area.
  • A WAN covers a large geographical area.
  • A LAN is often owned or managed by one organisation or user.
  • A WAN often uses external communication infrastructure.
  • A school network is an example of a LAN.
  • The Internet is an example of a WAN.
  • Accept any other valid point.

4) A school has computers connected together in one building. Explain why this is likely to be a LAN. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • The network covers a small geographical area.
  • The devices are located within one building or site.
  • The school can manage or control the network.
  • The network is not spread across a large geographical area.
  • Accept any other valid point.

5) A company has offices in London, Manchester and Cardiff connected together. Explain why this is likely to be a WAN. (3 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • The network connects sites across a large geographical area.
  • The offices are in different cities.
  • It may connect multiple LANs together.
  • It may use external communication infrastructure.
  • Accept any other valid point.

6) A school has 60 desktop computers connected in one building. Explain one advantage of using a LAN in this school. (2 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • Users can share resources such as printers or files.
  • The school can manage user accounts or security centrally.
  • Files can be stored or backed up centrally.
  • Users can access shared software or Internet access.
  • Accept any other valid point.

7) A company wants to connect offices in London, Cardiff and Glasgow. Explain one reason why this WAN may be more expensive to set up than a LAN. (2 marks)

Mark scheme

1 mark for each correct point:

  • A WAN covers a larger geographical area.
  • It may need telecommunications links or external infrastructure.
  • It may involve ongoing costs paid to network providers.
  • It may need more complex hardware, security or management.
  • Accept any other valid point.
